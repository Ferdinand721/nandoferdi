About Domain

  • A domain is an address or name that becomes the identity of a website or computer so that it can be accessed more easily by users.
  • For websites, domain names usually end with an extension such as .com or .net.
  • A domain extension is the final part of a website's domain name (such as .com, .org, .id) that indicates the site's category, purpose, or country of origin. This extension helps users identify the site's content type or origin, increasing trust and facilitating navigation.

    • Example

  • google.com
  • wikipedia.org
  • amazon.com
  • facebook.com
  • whitehouse.gov
  • harvard.edu
